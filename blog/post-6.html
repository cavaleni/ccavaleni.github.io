Post 6

Jan 16, 2021


Starting the process of changing or upgrading your wardrobe can be a terrifying task.
You have to get used to shopping in the men's’ clothing department if you haven’t before. You might feel nervous about wearing your new clothes for the first time in public. You might be afraid of what your family, friends, and even strangers may think.

However, it’s important to remember that you come first and it’s nobody’s life but yours. What they think doesn’t matter as long as it makes you happy. Just be sure that you’re in a safe situation to do so. Unfortunately, all of us aren’t lucky enough to not be in an abusive, nonsupportive situation.

If you’re ready to dive into the world of men’s clothes, I am providing some tips to get you started. Even if you’re not ready yet, this can be a useful article to read and save so you can get back to it when you are ready.

  1. Your style should be unique to you. It’ll take time to grow and develop as you grow as a person.

Everybody’s style is unique to them. If you see someone that always has on a fresh outfit, it didn’t happen overnight. I took years for them to develop that dope closet unless they’re pretty rich or a celebrity.

Some people I immediately think of are from Instagram. Jaceon Dido was the first black trans man that really caught my eye as far as fashion and fitness goals. He always has on the coolest outfits. At first, I told myself I would never have a wardrobe as dope and in-depth as his, but then I realized that he's been collecting shoes and clothes and hats for years and that’s why his collection is so extensive.

Another person I think of is BryantDigital, who’s fashion/personal page is Bryant.Lifestyle. I’ve been following him since my senior year of high school, but I found him on Tumblr first. He’s a great freelance photographer that does wedding events, family portraits, and graduation pictures.

One of the first things I noticed about him was that he loved luxurious high-end products like Gucci and Louis V, but he also loves streetwear and Harry Potter products. He has an insane collection of kicks, but he’s sold a lot of them to pay for his photography equipment.

His confidence stuck out to me. I admired it and wanted at least half the confidence he has. I keep up with him because he inspires me with his work ethic and fashion outfits. I enjoy seeing what he comes up.

It’s a good idea to find people on Pinterest and other social media to help you find out what you like and don’t like. Since I’ve been wearing men’s clothes since Elementary school, I already had a sense of what I liked and didn’t like, but these people still helped me tremendously.

2. Incorporate your interests and beliefs to stand out

Don’t be afraid to intertwine your personal interests and beliefs into your fashion purchases. As I’ve said before, Bryant has a vast collection of Harry Potter gear. Jaceon has a lot of NBA team gear and fitness clothing because he’s a personal trainer and he loves basketball.

Think of some of your interests and look for clothing pieces that represent them. I like video games, sports teams, and pro-LGBTQ and pro-black quotes and sayings. I incorporate all of these things in my collection because it gives people an idea of who I am right off the bat. I used to be afraid to wear LGBTQ/Trans stuff because I wasn’t exactly safe in Alabama, but once I got to Michigan and saw Ann Arbor I was comfortable enough to show my true self.

If you like bands, video games, and/or anime then you might be interested in Hot Topic and Spencers. If you love luxury clothes and can afford it, find a Neiman Marcus or a mall with luxury brand storefronts. I visited Neiman Marcus and Saks 5th Avenue and the prices stopped my heart, but they do have amazing products.

A lot of teens and young adults shop in places like CitiTrends, Rue 21, ASOS, Zumiez, Urban Outfitters, FashionNova, and many more. You can probably find something you like searching in one of these stores or checking out their online website. Most of these places also have great deals all year long, so signing up for their free memberships are always a good idea. You usually just need an email address or your cell phone number if you want texts.

3. Buy clothes that fit. (Both with and without a binder)

I used to only buy baggy clothes because I was insecure about my body, mainly due to not having top surgery yet. After I lost a lot of weight, I bought a binder that fit better and I was amazed at how flat it made my chest look.

I was “blessed” with a bigger chest, but with the new binder, it looks like I’ve already had top surgery. Combining that with how great my shape had become, I felt so much more confident. It took a long time to develop some confidence from having none, but it can be done.

It’s better to buy clothes that fit your body instead of hang off of it, but it’s 100% your choice. If you feel more comfortable in baggy clothing, by all means, keep buying them until you’re ready for more fitting items. It’s important to remember it’s an ongoing journey and not necessarily a true destination.

However, fitting clothes let people know that you’re confident in your body. More people will respect and admire you, even if your just walking around the grocery store. Seeing someone in baggy clothes makes people think you either don’t care how you look or are terribly insecure. People are nicer when they can’t figure out your vulnerability.

If you’re interested in talking to girls, then you definitely need some fitting clothes. If they can see your confidence, even if you have to fake it at first, it’ll get you a good first impression. It’s important to remember that women love a confident man. Every woman is different. Some of them prefer men with chubbier, softer stomachs. If they can feel safe and protected instead of feeling like they have to keep you protected in order to maintain your feelings, it can make a huge difference.

4. Practice self care and embrace your body no matter what type it is.

Acts of love and a good self-care routine go hand in hand with upgrading or redoing your wardrobe. As you start to take better care of yourself, your confidence and self-esteem will rise. It won’t be easy if you’re used to hearing a bullying voice in your head all the time, but it doesn’t last forever.

Try to change or negate every negative thought about yourself into a positive thought. If your mind tells you that you look fat, immediately counteract the statement with something about your appearance that you like — like your hairstyle or your smile.

If your mind tells you that you’re not good enough, start listing things that you are good at. Maybe you’re good at cooking, baking, drawing, singing, painting, or math. Maybe you get really good grades in highs school/college or do really well on essays or in science. Just find a couple of things you’re good at and enjoy and take a moment to recognize them, so you can realize how valuable you are.

This takes practice. You can also put words of affirmation on sticky notes and stick them to your mirror or the refrigerator or your bedroom walls. You can start a big jar by jotting down something you’re grateful for or compliments for yourself on little pieces of paper, and then pull some out to read when you’re feeling bad.

Selfcare is proper hygiene and making good choices for yourself. I’m not one of those people that’s going to try to shame you into exercising, but if that’s something you’re interested in please don’t let other people talk you out of trying. If you pay for a gym membership or buy a fitness dvd/subscription then or even go to a free gym, then you deserve to be able to peacefully participate and be present.

Other people don’t get to tell you where you don’t belong. I personally hate when people make viral videos of unfit people and mistakes then post them on the internet so everyone can laugh. That hurts the poor stranger they’ve exposed and it convinces them not to step foot in a gym ever again.

If you happen to notice or think someone might be filming you, don’t be scared to confront them. You can even get a worker involved if you need to. If you were wrong, apologize but remain firm. If someone tries to bully you, don’t be afraid to try to stick up for yourself or go find a worker.

Employees are obligated to keep you safe while you’re in their facility, so if they brush you off or give you a negative response make sure to get their name from their name tag or badge and report them to someone in charge of them. If they get reprimanded or even fired, it is not your fault because they weren’t doing their job.

5. Brand names and high prices don’t always mean top quality.

High prices and brand names can determine how much quality a product has, but simply using those two things doesn’t always get you accurate results. Take Armani or Tom Ford suits for example. They’re both good brands. Tom Ford is significantly less than Armani, but it’s still in the hundreds. If you can’t afford it, that’s completely okay. Go into a less expensive store and feel the products and try them on.

Use your experience satisfaction and touch. This will tell you about the quality of something. Don’t be afraid to ask someone that works there for recommendations and state your budget. Don’t let them talk you into spending an extra couple of hundreds above your budget either.

The same thing goes for streetwear and casual wear. Is Gucci nice? Sure, but it’s terribly expensive. $300 for one T-shirt expensive! You can get a quality brand graphic T-shirt for way less. Check out Zumies, Karmaloop, other shops like them, or some small online clothing lines, and even Etsy. These sites almost always have deals going on like “Buy One Get One Free” or “25–50% off your entire order” and even constant sales on specific items.

Look at other peoples’ reviews since you can’t touch them yourself if you’re on the website. Maybe ask a trusted male friend where they like to shop, or you can even ask influencers and community on social media apps/sites for help. You can hop on a transgender Reddit community and post a question, then you’ll certainly get a lot of help.

It is important to remember that this is about you and for you only.

This is your journey and your decisions are solely yours. Sure you can ask friends, family members, and strangers for their opinions but you absolutely don’t have to take their advice. Personally, my grandmother and my adopted parents tell me what they think looks good on me and I can count on them to be honest. However, all decisions are finalized by me unless it’s a gift.

Have fun and don’t be afraid to explore!
